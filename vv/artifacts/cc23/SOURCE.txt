CC-23 — workspace personalization (settings, dotfiles, secrets) authority artifacts.

A Codespace/Gitpod carries an operator's personalization — their editor settings,
their dotfiles, and their secrets — so their environment follows them into any
workspace. holospaces realizes this WITHOUT a server account: a Personalization
is a hologram Realization — IRI-tagged canonical bytes that embed the operator
identity (the κ of their self-sovereign key, CC-1/CC-12) and carry the
settings/dotfiles/secrets as payload. The whole personalization is therefore
itself content — a κ scoped to the operator (the same content under a different
operator is a different κ), held in the store and synced by the substrate's
KappaSync, not by any host (Laws L1/L3; ADR-001). On entry holospaces applies it:
the dotfiles are injected into the devcontainer OS's home directory and the
secrets are exported into its environment by an entry /init, and the editor
settings are handed to the workbench.

Authorities
-----------
Dev Container specification — remoteEnv / secrets exposed to the environment. The
  operator's secrets are exported into the devcontainer OS's environment by the
  entry /init (as a Codespace exposes its secrets as env vars).

Codespaces / Gitpod dotfiles convention — dotfiles placed in $HOME. The operator's
  dotfiles are injected into the home directory (/root/<name>) of the booted OS.

ext4 on-disk format — e2fsprogs (e2fsck/debugfs) is the oracle that the operator's
  dotfiles and the entry /init are present in the assembled rootfs and read back
  byte-identically (the rootfs the OS boots).

holospaces' own emulator — the substrate the holospace actually boots on. It boots
  the assembled rootfs, execs the entry /init, and applies the personalization
  under a real libc (busybox) shell: SECRET-PRESENT:<name> confirms the secret is
  in the environment (the value is NOT printed — secrets are confirmed present,
  not leaked), and DOTFILE-PRESENT:<name> + the dotfile's content confirms the
  operator's dotfile is in the home directory.

Artifacts
---------
The shell base is the SAME published BusyBox riscv64 image as CC-22
(vv/artifacts/cc22/image — statically-linked BusyBox v1.37.0; see
vv/artifacts/cc22/SOURCE.txt for its pin and provenance). CC-23 does not
duplicate it: the personalization is the operator's OWN content, layered over the
same shell-bearing OS. No new external artifact is imported — the personalization
content is synthesized in the witness from an operator identity, a .gitconfig
dotfile, editor settings, and a GH_TOKEN secret.

The CC-14 kernel (vv/artifacts/cc14/kernel/Image.gz) is reused for the emulator
boot, as in CC-22.

Witness — crates/holospaces/tests/cc23_personalization.rs; suite
vv/suites/cc23-personalization.sh:
  unit (personalization module) — the personalization is content scoped to the
      operator: the same content + same operator yields the same κ (reproducible),
      a different operator yields a different κ (scoped); it round-trips through
      its canonical form; the entry /init exports the secrets and confirms the
      dotfiles; home_files place the dotfiles under the home directory;
  (1) the operator's dotfiles + entry /init are injected into the assembled ext4
      rootfs — e2fsck clean and debugfs reads /root/.gitconfig AND /init back
      byte-identically (the rootfs the OS boots to apply the personalization);
  (2) holospaces' own emulator applies the personalization under a real libc
      (busybox) shell — SECRET-PRESENT:GH_TOKEN (the secret is in the OS
      environment, value not leaked) and DOTFILE-PRESENT:.gitconfig + its content
      (the operator's dotfile is in the home directory). #[ignore]d (heavy, a
      real-OS boot to userland).

Note (workbench settings)
-------------------------
The operator's editor settings are carried as part of the κ-addressed
personalization (the settings JSON is in the canonical form, witnessed by the
round-trip). Handing the settings to the running workbench is the web path
(CC-13/CC-17 load a settings.json into the real workbench); this CC witnesses the
content + identity scoping and the OS-side application (dotfiles + secrets).
